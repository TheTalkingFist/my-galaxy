# Act I - Meet me at the training grounds!

"Say, Ash..." Akari started.

"Yeah?"

Ash and Bidoof were finishing up on their training, the Pokemon looking exhausted, yet still fairly satisfied with itself.

"I might need your help with something, after this. Could you meet me at the training grounds?"

"Uh..." Ash trailed off, "Where's that, again?"

Right, she hasn't told him. "You know, when you're exiting the Galaxy Hall? Turn right, it's in the fenced area." Ash nodded. "Great, thanks! I'll be there."

<hr>

'I didn't tell him a time, but surely he isn't taking this long...'

"Sorry, Akari! I got lost!"

At first, she thought he was joking. "How the heck...? It's literally at the end of the pathway, how?"

Ash had no answer other than a sheepish look, shared with Pikachu. Akari decided it wasn't worth questioning any further. "No matter! L-Listen, I've had an epiphany! Remember how I said I have a Pikachu?"

Ash nodded. "Good. I realised that both of us, we need to work together to get our Pokédex finished! But, like I told you, Pikachu and I don't really get along all that well... I don't even know how to order him in battle, to be honest with you..."

Suddenly, Akari snapped her head up with a determined expression. "So please! Let's have a battle!"

Ash stood there for a few moments, before breaking into a grin. "Now, you're talkin' my language!"

<hr>

The two of them separated into opposite sides of the battlefield, staring at one another. Akari threw a Pokeball and it opened with a ping, a Pikachu materialising out of it. It looked around, assessed the situation... and sighed.

Ash and Pikachu felt a bead of sweat dropping down their faces. "Not very interested, huh...?" The boy deadpanned. "That's fine! We'll show you how we do it! Pikachu?"

Ash's Pikachu ran up to the field.

"Pikachu!" Akari said, "On the field! Now!"

The Pikachu only looked back, sighing and reluctantly stepping forward.

# Act II - Pikachu V Pikachu

"Akari, you can have the first move!" Ash called out. She huffed, but took the opportunity regardless, stretching her arm out and commanding,

"Alright, Pikachu, Thunder Shock, now!"

...

"Ka..." The Pikachu seemed to huff, smirking as it gathered its power. "Ka-CHUUUU!!" It cried, letting out a large shock of electricity to its opponent, covering them in a cloud of sand and grit. Once it cleared...

"Chu..." Ash's Pikachu stood perfectly fine, rubbing its neck, Ash doing the same thing.

"Pika?! Ka-CHUUUUUUUU!!!" It tried an even stronger Thunder Shock, achieving the same effect.

"Oh, boy..." Ash deadpanned. At this point, Akari couldn't even face him.

"Pika! Pika-pika!" Akari's Pikachu called out in a tone that Ash and his Pikachu could only assume as 'mocking'.

"Right, well... Thunderbolt?" Ash said. His Pikachu followed, charging up a Thunderbolt attack.

On the other side of the field, the opposing Electric Mouse smirked, charging up and shooting out a Thunder Shock meant to catch his opponent off-guard while he was charging up his attack..!

Only for the Thunderbolt to release much faster than he could expect. The two bolts of electricity travelled at light speed, meeting each other in the center...

And the Thunderbolt shattered the weaker Thunder Shock, breaking it utterly and hitting Akari's Pikachu dead center, launching the Pokemon past the gates.

Akari couldn't even register what had happened as her Pikachu flew past her. In the few moments that led, part of her wished she'd never offered a battle. 'Gosh, I'm so thankful nobody else is here. Still... am I really that weak...?'

The rustle of fabric snapped her from her thoughts and she looked up to see Ash offer a handshake, which she reluctantly took.

"Good fight!" Ash smiled.

"Was it really..?" Akari deadpanned.

"Uh... well..." Ash's eyes shifted away.

Akari sighed. "Forget it, this is useless." She turned away, after recalling Pikachu into her Pokeball.

"H-Hey, Akari, Wait!"

# Act III - I Wish I Were Stronger

Akari leaned against the outer fence of the town. She knew she was going to lose; she's seen firsthand the power of his Pikachu, as well as the ingenuity of the boy commanding it. It would take nothing short of intervention at the ends of Almighty Sinnoh for her to have a standing chance.

Still... It sucked losing that quickly. Especially after that embarrassing display.

She let out another sigh, squatting down and hugging her knees. Just as her mind started wandering to find any alternative to her problem...

"Oh, there you are!"

Akari jumped, turning to her right to see the very boy that decimated her in battle.

"Oh... hi." Akari winced. She didn't mean for it to come out so coldly.

"Hey," Ash smiled. If he noticed her tone, he didn't show it, instead just sitting on the empty patch beside her.

For a while, they didn't say anything. Akari's eyes were glued to her feet, while Ash stared at the open path in front of him.

"You know, I had another Pokemon where I came from?" Ash said after a few minutes, startling Akari.
